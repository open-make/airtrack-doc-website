Construction of the air table center

Step 1: Predrill of plexiglass plates

  • Drill several holes (0.8 cm diameter) into the 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate as shown in the following drawing.

    • The given distances in the first picture can be transferred to all other holes.

  • After drilling remove remains of drilling.

Now you have a predrilled 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate.

To connect the central box with the table legs the long side of the central box needs connection points.

  • Therefore drill a hole on each end of both 480mm x 64mm plexiglass plates- {Qty:2} with a 5mm plexiglas drill.

    • Keep a distance of 4.5 cm to the end edge and 1 cm to the top.

  • Drill two more holes inside both 480mm x 64mm plexiglass plates with a distance of 12.5cm towards the plate's center.

Step 2: Cleaning the plexiglass

Before beginning any gluing work, the pieces must be degreased (soap and water) and without any remaining screwing or other dirt.

Step 3: Glueing

The first step is to build the central plexiglas box where the air is floating through.

  • Use the acrylic glue to combine the predrilled 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate with two 310mm x 64mm plexiglass plates.

    • These are placed on the shorter edge of the 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate with a space of 8mm on both sides. You are able to mark the corner spaces with a pen on the 8mm height of the largest plexiglas plate.

  • Then use the arcylic glue to connect the components.

  • Now use the arcylic glue to add the 480mm x 64mm plexiglass plates.

    • Don't forget to put glue between both plexiglass components with 6.4 cm height.

You now have the upper side of the air table center. The bottom plate will be glued later.

Step 4: Screwing

To further stabilize the box you need to drill screws from the side into the box from several places.

  • Every place for a screw must be predrilled with a 1mm plexiglas drill.

  • Start with the long sides, predrill from the top into the upper plexiglas with a distance of 5.9 cm between each screw.

    • The corner hole has a center distance of 4mm on both sides.

  • Continue with the short upper side, keep a distance of 5 cm between holes. The center hole has a distance of 5.9 cm to both neighbor screws

Now drill 3mm pozidriv screws in each hole.

The bottom plate is meant to disconnect first if the pressure in the box gets too high. Therefore the bottom plate is not screwed down.

Step 5: Inserting of hose connection gland and pressure relief valve

These components take care of the incoming air and protect against overpressure.

  • Choose one short side of the box and drill in the center of the the plexiglass a hole with a G 1/4" thread drill.

  • Now insert the reducing fitting. Place with distance of 7,5cm two more threads with a G 1/8" thread drill on both side of the last thread.

  • Place inside these threads the hose connection glands. You can place on the reducing fitting the pressure relief valve.

    • It's recommended to remove it during works on the Airtable.

  • Further combine two 20cm compressed air hose with an Y-hose connector.

    • Append to the in one direction pointing end of the Y-hose connector another compressed air hose with the length of your need.

  • Connect both 20cm compressed air hose with the hose connection glands.

You have build the air table center box now.
