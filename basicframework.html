Construction of the framework

Parts

  • 1 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate
  • 1 air table center box
  • 3 aluminium strut profiles (length: 286mm)
  • 4 aluminium strut profiles (length: 400mm)
  • 2 aluminium strut profiles (length: 440mm)
  • 2 aluminium strut profiles (length: 480mm)
  • 12 angle
  • 2 angles
  • 26 M5 screw (6mm)
  • 4 M5x12mm screws
  • 14 nut
  • 18 nuts

Now you're going to fix the air table center box with a surrounding framework, so it stands.

Step 1: U-formation framework

We will first build two U-shaped framework which will be connected to the airtable using the holes we prepared during the predrilling stage. Accordingly the central part of the "U" should be 480 mm. You can decide of the length of the other two elements that will become the feets of the table. We usually use 400 mm, and if should not be less than 250 mm to get enough distance for the camera fixed below.

  • Place one nut inside each of four aluminium strut profiles (length: 400mm) used as feets.
  • Fixate on each an angle with a M5 screw (6mm) at one end of the strut.
  • Do this twice: Connect two of the four struts with a nut and angle with one of the 480 mm long aluminium strut profiles (length: 480mm) by using nuts and a M5 screw (6mm).

Step 2: Fixing the U-formation framework to the central air table

In this set we will attach the blue marked frame to the air table box.

  • Place two nuts inside each 480 mm rod of the U-formation frameworks on one side which adjacent to the side with nuts already placed in (90 degrees to both rods which can vary in length), both sides work.

  • Now line these nuts up with the holes inside the long sides of the air table (480mm)

  • The holes in the 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate of the airtable need to point in the opposite direction as the two rods which can vary in length.

  • Connect the air table box and the U-formation framework with two M5x12mm screws from the inside of the air box in each nut.

Repeat this step with the second U-formation framework on the other side of the air table box.

Step 3: Attach a second frame circle

To guarantee a higher stability it's recommended to place a second framework circle 5-10cm above the ground. The second framework circle is shown in the picture below.

Picture of the second frame circle

  • Place on both ends of the aluminium strut profiles (length: 440mm) and on both ends of the aluminium strut profiles (length: 286mm) a nut.

  • Put an extra nut in both 440 mm aluminium strut profiles. It must point to the center later (90Â° shifted from the other two nuts).

  • Connect the nuts and an angle with a M5 screw (6mm).

  • Place nuts at the wished height inside the to the Airtable mounted aluminium strut profile. The nuts need to point towards the other vertical struts.

  • Now connect the aluminium strut profiles with angles to the at the Airtable mounted aluminium strut profile with a M5 screw (6mm).

  • Put two nuts inside the third of the aluminium strut profiles (length: 286mm). These nuts need to point upwards in the next step.

  • Place the third 326 mm aluminium strut profile in the center at the two unused nuts and connect it with two angles and two more M5 screw (6mm).

Step 4: Glueing of the bottom plate

After connecting the air table with the framework there is no need in accessing the inner box.

  • Again it's necessary to degrease and remove any dirt at the glueing surface before glueing.

  • Glue the second 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate on the bottom side of the already build.

Step 5: Glue bottom plate

The bottom plate is meant to disconnect first if the pressure in the box gets too high. Therefore the bottom plate is not screwed.

  • Line up the long side of the box with the long side of the plate, there must be no hole for air flowing through.

  • Glue the not predrilled bottom 480mm x 326mm plexiglass plate below the box.

    • It's recommended to put the glue on the 8 mm wide sides.

Wait for the glue to be dry before going to the next step.

You have build the center air table now.
